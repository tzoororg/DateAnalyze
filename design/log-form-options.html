Log form — 4 redesign options

All four fix the same complaints, each in its own way: photos come first and anchor the whole flow · mood list is gone (replaced by a free word, free emoji, or nothing) · cost is categorical (Free / $ / $$ / $$$) · enjoyment + "again?" merged into one question · fields cut down so nothing scrolls (effort, location, notes are dropped or demoted everywhere).

Option 1 — The Polaroid

One screen, zero scrolling. The photo IS the form: a polaroid you fill, with the caption handwritten on its bottom edge (that's the title). Below it just four quick controls. Rating: 1–5 hearts with a verdict word (1 = "never again", 5 = "new favorite") — one tap answers both old questions. Mood → "one word for the vibe": free text, with your own past words as suggestions, so the vocabulary grows with you instead of a fixed list.

Option 2 — The Wizard

Three full-screen steps, so each screen is tiny — no scrolling ever. Step 1 is only the photo. From step 2 on, your photo becomes the background of the whole form, so the process literally surrounds it. Rating: a single "again-o-meter" slider from 😵 never again to 😍 again ASAP. Mood → react with any emoji from the full keyboard, like reacting to a message — no fixed list to not-fit.

Option 3 — The Chat

Logging as a conversation — the app asks, you tap quick replies. Feels like texting a friend about the date, not filling a form. Opens by demanding the photo ("pics or it didn't happen"). Rating: one face — 😴 to 😍 — answers how it was AND whether you'd repeat. Mood is gone entirely: the free-text "anything to remember?" carries the vibe. Category is auto-guessed from your answer, one tap to fix. Every question skippable.

Option 4 — The Scrapbook

The photo fills the page and you decorate it with stickers — nothing looks like a form field. Caption + date are handwritten tape strips; rating is a hearts sticker (1–5, wording doubles as the repeat verdict); cost is a price-tag sticker; category is a badge sticker. Mood → slap any emoji stickers on the photo. The tray at the bottom is the whole "form"; everything lands on the photo itself.
