Concepts 1–3 (first round — “three redesign concepts”)

All three: kill the enjoyment/would-repeat overlap (one verdict control), replace the fixed mood list, make cost categorical or gone, and fit on one screen. Each takes a different philosophy about what logging a date should feel like.

Option 1 — “Snap”: one screen, expand if you care

Philosophy: a date log is a 15-second chore — optimize for speed. Everything essential fits without scrolling: title + date on one line, categories as a swipeable emoji strip, one 4-button verdict (merges enjoyment + would-repeat), cost as Free/$/$$/$$$ pills. Mood chips are gone — if you want to say more, “Add more” reveals a free-text vibe field, location and notes. Most saves never open it.

Concepts 4–7 (second round — “four directions”, photo-first)

Your four complaints, and where each concept lands:
① too long / too much scrolling · ② moods never fit · ③ numeric cost is awkward · ④ enjoyment ≈ would-repeat (double question)
Option 4

Polaroid card — everything on one screen

The whole form is one screen, zero scrolling. The photo is a tilted polaroid at the top and the title is handwritten on it as the caption. Below: one row of categories, one verdict row, one cost row. Notes, location and extra photos hide behind a fold.

① scrollingeverything fits one screen; extras collapsed behind "add note…"
② moodremoved entirely — the verdict face carries the feeling
③ costsegmented Free / $ / $$ / $$$
④ double questionone 4-face verdict: Again! / Good / Meh / Nope (maps to enjoyment 5/4/3/1 + repeat yes/maybe/no)
Option 5

Three-step story — photo gets a full screen

A quick wizard: each step is tiny, so nothing ever scrolls. Step 1 is only the photo — a near full-screen well. Step 2 is the facts. Step 3 is the feelings, with a single 0–10 dial and free-typed vibe words instead of a fixed mood list (your own past words come back as suggestions, so the vocabulary grows to fit you).

① scrollingthree screens, each fits without scrolling; 2 taps + typing total
② moodfreeform vibe words you type ("cozy", "rainy", "tipsy") — old picks become suggestion chips
③ costremoved entirely
④ double questionone 0–10 dial "How was it?" — 8+ implies "again", ≤4 implies "nope"
Option 6

Chat check-in — the app interviews you

Logging feels like texting a friend after the date. The app opens by asking for the photo, then asks one tiny question at a time with tap-to-answer chips — you never see a form, and there is nothing to scroll because there is only ever one question on screen. Ends with a summary card you confirm.

① scrollingno form at all — one question at a time, most answered with one tap
② mood"one word for tonight?" — a single free-typed word, no list to not-fit
③ costasked as a chip question: Free / $ / $$ / $$$ (tap "skip" and it never asks again)
④ double questionhearts 1–5, one question; 4–5 hearts auto-marks "do it again", 1–2 marks "nope"
Option 7

Scrapbook page — decorate the photo itself

The photo becomes the page, and every field is a sticker you slap onto it. Category sticker, price-tag sticker, a vibe sticker that is any emoji from the full keyboard (no fixed list), and a wax-stamp verdict. The title is handwritten across the photo. What you build here is literally what the History gallery shows later.

① scrollingzero — the photo is the whole screen, the sticker tray sits at the bottom
② moodvibe sticker = any emoji on your keyboard — infinite vocabulary, no list
③ costa price-tag sticker: FREE / $ / $$ / $$$ (skip it = no cost logged)
④ double questionone stamp: KEEPER / NICE / ONE-OFF — pressed like a wax seal onto the corner

Concepts 8–11 (third round — “4 redesign options”)

All four fix the same complaints, each in its own way: photos come first and anchor the whole flow · mood list is gone (replaced by a free word, free emoji, or nothing) · cost is categorical (Free / $ / $$ / $$$) · enjoyment + "again?" merged into one question · fields cut down so nothing scrolls (effort, location, notes are dropped or demoted everywhere).

Option 8 — The Polaroid

One screen, zero scrolling. The photo IS the form: a polaroid you fill, with the caption handwritten on its bottom edge (that's the title). Below it just four quick controls. Rating: 1–5 hearts with a verdict word (1 = "never again", 5 = "new favorite") — one tap answers both old questions. Mood → "one word for the vibe": free text, with your own past words as suggestions, so the vocabulary grows with you instead of a fixed list.

Logging as a conversation — the app asks, you tap quick replies. Feels like texting a friend about the date, not filling a form. Opens by demanding the photo ("pics or it didn't happen"). Category is auto-guessed from your answer, one tap to fix. Every question skippable.
